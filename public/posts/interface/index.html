People like Ada Loveace set the standards for modern computing. During World War 2, the germans developed the Engima Machine, rasing the bar of encryption. British geniuses reunited and developed an algorithm to break the cypher, if done manually would take constant days of work, bad thing that the Germans changed the hash every 12 hours.

Alan Turing came up with an idea, what if we develop a new kind of machine whose instructions weren’t hardcoded in a sense into it. The machine would need to have the programs loaded into typically by magnetic tape, etc. A completly new world of things could be done, more efficiently and fast. Worth mentioning that this miracle hardware (Named the Turing Machine) evolved into what we know today as a “Computer”.
